2. The 4 Cases (Kasus)
The article changes depending on the role of the noun in the sentence.
Nominative (The Subject - Who acts?)
The "Standard" form. Used for the person doing the action.
Der Mann isst den Apfel.
Accusative (The Object - Who is acted upon?)
Used for the direct object. Only Masculine changes!
Der Mann isst den Apfel. (Der -> Den)
Dative (The Receiver - To whom?)
Used for the receiver or after specific prepositions (mit, bei, von).
Ich gebe dem Mann ein Buch.

3. Sentence Structure
Rule 1: Verb is ALWAYS Position 2
In a normal main clause, the conjugated verb stays at position 2. No matter what comes first.
1. Ich gehe heute ins Kino.
2. Heute gehe ich ins Kino.
Rule 2: Verb Kick (Separable)
If a verb has a prefix (e.g. aufstehen), the prefix gets kicked to the end.
Ich stehe jeden Morgen um 6 Uhr auf.
